Light Pollution

Dim the lights, save the night!

About Light Pollution

Light pollution is the overuse of artificial light hat brightens the night sky, reducing our ability to see stars and disrupting natural ecosystems. It affects wildlife by interfering with migration patterns, sleep cycles, and predator-prey relationships, especially for nocturnal animals. Humans also experience negative effects, such as disrupted sleep due to exposure to artificial light at night, which can impact overall health. By using shielded lighting, reducing unnecessary brightness, and promoting awareness, we can help restore the natural beauty of the night sky while protecting both wildlife and human well-being.

Causes of light pollution:

Glare

Uncomfortably bright lights that directly shine into the eye

Light Trespass

Unintended light that spills into other people's spaces

Skyglow

Light scattered into the atmosphere that brightens the night sky

Light Clutter

Excessive or confusing groupings of light sources

Let's look at the facts...

Levels of light pollution in the U.S. have increased rapidly over the years.

Starlight is greatly diminished, even in rural and suburban areas, impacting both people and wildlife.

Light pollution is a global problem that requires a global solution.

Impact!

Lasting Effects of Light Pollution on Wildlife

A heavily light polluted sky.

Disrupted Sleep and Circadian Rhythms

  • Artificial light can disrupt natural day-night cycles.
  • Birds using moonlight to migrate may get lost.

Disorientation and Poor Navigation

  • Light pollution confuses animal navigation.
  • Sea turtle hatchlings may head towards road lights instead of the ocean.

Attraction to Artificial Lights

  • Insects drawn to artificial light may become prey.
  • Billions of nocturnal insects are killed annually by artificial lights.

Reduced Survival and Reproduction

  • Light pollution affects predator-prey dynamics.
  • It alters food webs and reduces reproductive success.
Lasting Effects of Light Pollution on Ecosystems

Impacts on Predation & Reproduction

  • Artificial light attracts prey, making them easy targets.
  • Disrupted rhythms affect breeding success.

Impacts on Plant Life

  • Artificial light can lead to premature budding.
  • Nocturnal pollinators become disoriented, reducing pollination.

Impacts on Habitat Loss

  • Bright areas become uninhabitable for certain species.
  • Divides habitats into smaller, isolated sections.
Lasting Effects of Light Pollution on Humans

Melatonin Disruption & Sleep Problems

  • Artificial light at night reduces melatonin production.
  • Can lead to insomnia and poor sleep quality.

Circadian Rhythm Disruption

  • Impacts hormone regulation and mental health.
  • Can increase obesity risk.

Potential Health Concerns

  • Links to depression, cardiovascular disease, and cancers.
  • Night shift work exposure is associated with higher health risks.
